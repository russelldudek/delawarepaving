## Color token provenance
Source-sampled from the official logo:
- DVP red `#ED1C24`
- DVP cyan-blue `#00AFEF`
- White `#FFFFFF`

Environmental neutrals are derived from asphalt, aggregate, base stone, and soil cues. Candidate-original signal amber `#F3A526` is used sparingly for readiness and caution states; it is not represented as an official company color.

## Typography evidence and decision
The public identity combines a distinctive script logo with bold, compact, high-contrast sans-serif web typography. No public font license or official type standard was identified. The campaign therefore uses installed/open `Noto Sans Display` for condensed display hierarchy and `Noto Sans` / Roboto / Liberation Sans fallbacks for body text. No proprietary font files are copied or committed.

## Photography / interface / geometry character
- Saturated jobsite and equipment photography.
- High contrast red, cyan-blue, white, and asphalt-dark surfaces.
- Practical, operations-forward language.
- Roadway lines, material layers, aggregate texture, and industrial joints.
- Tight headline typography with straightforward supporting copy.

## Role-to-brand translation
The campaign's original visual metaphor is a pavement cross-section under a moving compaction load. It translates the role mandate into five load-bearing workflow layers: field truth, work ownership, data continuity, system joints, and decision surface. This is candidate-created and does not imitate a Delaware Valley application or internal dashboard.

## Independent-candidate distinction
Every route identifies the work as Russell Dudek's independent candidate vision and includes a non-endorsement statement. Official marks are used only to identify the employer.
